Alexander Fleming

The person who discovered penicillin and saved millions of people from dying with bacterial infections

Fleming's Biography:

  • He was born in Scotland on August 6, 1881,
  • Fleming was a member of the Territorial Army, and served from 1900 to 1914 in the London Scottish Regiment
  • He won the 1908 gold medal as the top medical student.
  • Through research and experimentation, Fleming discovered a bacteria-destroying mold which he would call penicillin in 1928
  • He was awarded the Nobel Prize in 1945.
  • He died on March 11, 1955
